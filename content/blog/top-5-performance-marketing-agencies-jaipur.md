## What "performance marketing agency" actually means

A real performance marketing agency:

- **Treats every rupee as accountable** — not in a "we'll send you reports" way, but in a "every campaign rolls up to a measurable cost-per-action" way.
- **Optimizes for the bottom of the funnel** — they care about cost per signed customer, not cost per click.
- **Can read attribution despite iOS 17 / cookie deprecation** — server-side tracking, conversions API, post-purchase attribution surveys, modeled conversions.
- **Will say no to bad campaigns** — they don't run brand-awareness videos for an SMB unless that's the right move; they push back when they see budget being burned.

If an agency uses the words "performance marketing" but doesn't do the four things above, they're a digital agency wearing a different hat. The five below actually do.

## What to ask in your first call with any of them

The interview process for hiring a performance marketing agency should be more rigorous than the one you'd run for a senior hire. You're handing them six- or seven-figure annual budgets. Five questions that cut through agency pitch theater:

**1. "Walk me through how you'd attribute a sale that came from a Meta ad click 14 days ago."**
Good agencies talk through Conversions API, server-side events, deduplication logic, and modeled conversions. Bad agencies talk about pixel events.

**2. "What's the biggest campaign you've personally optimized in the last 90 days, and what did it teach you?"**
You want concrete operational stories. Not "we got 5x ROAS for a client."

**3. "How does AI show up in your workflow today, specifically?"**
"We use ChatGPT for captions" is a fail. "We have an AI lead-scoring layer that filters leads pre-qualified before reaching sales" is a pass.

**4. "What's your weekly reporting cadence and what's actually in it?"**
Live dashboard + weekly Loom = good. 30-page PDF every Friday = a tell that they don't have real-time tooling.

**5. "If we hire you and three months later we want to leave, what do we walk away with?"**
Everything (ad accounts, landing pages, pixels, content library, audience seeds) = good. "We'll transition you" with no specifics = exit-cost trap.

## Performance vs. brand: it's not always either-or

One nuance worth noting. The five agencies above are performance-first. That's right for ~80% of growing businesses. But businesses that have hit a ceiling with pure-performance — usually around the ₹5L/month spend mark, depending on category — often need to layer brand marketing on top to keep growing efficiently.

When you're evaluating performance agencies, ask whether they can also handle the brand layer when you need it (in-house creative team, video production, PR partnerships) or whether you'll need a second agency for that. Most can't handle both well; agencies that claim to are usually mediocre at both.
