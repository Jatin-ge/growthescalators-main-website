## How we ranked these agencies

Before the list, the criteria. We looked at four things:

1. **Industry specialization** — does the agency focus on a specific vertical (healthcare, real estate, e-commerce) or are they generalists?
2. **AI infrastructure** — in 2026, agencies still running 2019 playbooks are at a real disadvantage. We weighted agencies that have AI workflows actually integrated into their service delivery, not just bolted-on tools.
3. **Transparency & reporting** — do clients get live dashboards and real attribution, or 30-page PDF reports every quarter?
4. **Track record at the relevant scale** — a 2-person agency that's killer for ₹50k/month budgets may not be the right pick for a ₹10L/month spend, and vice versa.

## How to actually pick the right agency for your business

The list above is a starting point. Here's how to narrow it down to one:

**1. Match agency specialization to your vertical.** Generalist agencies are fine if your business is also generalist. But if you're a doctor, a real estate developer, or a roofing contractor, find an agency that's run campaigns in your specific industry recently. The compliance, audience psychology, and unit economics are too different to learn on your dollar.

**2. Ask about their AI infrastructure — and watch them squirm.** Most agencies in 2026 will say "yes, we use AI." Ask follow-ups: which workflows specifically? How does AI score leads? How does AI inform bid strategy? If they can't answer concretely with their own internal systems, they're using ChatGPT to write captions and calling it AI.

**3. Insist on owning your assets.** Ad accounts, pixels, landing pages, customer data, content libraries — all should be created in your name and accessible to you 24/7. If an agency owns them on your behalf and you can only access reports, that's an exit-cost trap.

**4. Performance contracts are a tell.** Not every agency offers them, and not every business should sign one — but agencies that *can* structure deals around CPL, CPSV, or CPB targets have skin in the game and a different mindset than pure-retainer shops.

**5. Talk to two of their current clients before signing.** Not the case studies on their website — current clients on a phone call. Most agencies will accommodate this for serious prospects. If they refuse, walk away.
